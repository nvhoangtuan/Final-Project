London is one of the world's most-visited cities, home to three UNESCO World Heritage sites and countless more palaces, museums, and monuments.

Discover more sights in less time on a hop-on hop-off bus journey, travel the city by classic black cab, or sightseeing by bike.

You can book guided visits to landmarks like the Tower of London and Westminster Abbey and neighborhood tours of literary Bloomsbury or edgy Shoreditch. And don't forget the day trips to Windsor Castle, Stonehenge, or Bath.

Tower of London and Crown Jewels Exhibition

Overview

Enjoy the Tower of London and make the most of your entry ticket on a tour of this dark and infamous complex with a Beefeater. View the remarkable Crown Jewels and visit the White Tower, Traitors' Gate, and Tower Green, where Anne Boleyn was beheaded. Follow a Beefeater guard on a walk to learn insider stories about this remarkable, UNESCO World Heritage Site that habors almost 1,000 years of history.

  1. View the Crown Jewels, a highlight of the Tower of London
  2. Enjoy a complimentary Yeoman Warder (Beefeater) tour - The first tour starts at 10:00 from Tuesday - Saturday and at 10:30 on Sunday and Monday.
  3. Get up close and personal with a UNESCO World Heritage Site

The London Eye

Overview

One of London's most recognizable and in-demand attractions, the Lastminute.com London Eye welcomes millions of visitors every year. Save yourself any sightseeing hassle, and secure your admission before you travel, when you book your tickets in advance. Upon arrival, step into a large, air-conditioned capsule and take to the skies. The Lastminute.com London Eye stands at 443 feet (135 meters) high, and offers impeccable views of Big Ben, St. Paul's Cathedral, Buckingham Palace, and other key landmarks.

  1. Choose a time slot that works best for your sightseeing schedule
  2. Prevent any travel hassles by securing your admission in advance
  3. Enjoy panoramic views of London, and spot its key landmarks from the sky
  4. Get great photo opportunities from high in the sky

Wembley Stadium in London

Overview

With our experienced guide and web-based app, take an immersive tour of Wembley Stadium. Walk where your heroes have played, performed and celebrated, including the Women's England team during their Euros success and take in the atmosphere of one of the most famous stadiums in the world. Visit the player's tunnel and the pitch, see the dressing rooms, and get a look inside the Royal Box. A must-see for any football, NFL or music fan.
